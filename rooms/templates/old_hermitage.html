SOON The Sculptural Figure Of A Bull

The sculptural figures of bulls are important archeological finds that date back to the early fourth millennium B.C. They are made of gold, approximately 10-inches tall, and were discovered in tombs belonging to kings of the Minoan civilization, which ruled over the region. Since its discovery, they have been studied and analyzed by archeologists, who believe that these figures may have served as objects of worship or symbols of fertility for ancient people.

SOON The Stamped Deer Plate

The stamped deer plate is a gold-made artefact discovered during archeological digs in the end of 7th c. BC. It measures approximately 30 cm in diameter and was likely used in religious ceremonies. Today, the plate is displayed in museums and is considered a valuable and rare piece of history.

SOON The Stamped Panther Plate

The stamped panther plate was found during excavations in 7th-century BC in the area of modern-day Turkey. It was made of gold and enamel, with amber inlays. The artefact was believed to have been used as a sacred object in ancient times, possibly as a part of religious rituals. The stamped panther artefacts were found in the tombs of the wealthy and powerful in ancient Mesopotamia.

Today, these artefacts are preserved in archaeological museums around the world, where they continue to fascinate scholars and the general public alike.

EUPHRONIA’S PSYCTER

The Attic red-figure psykter of the strict work style of Euphronius, featuring four hetaerae, is a clay-made historical artefact dating back to the 6th to 4th centuries BC. It was found in an Athenian temple and is now housed at the Old Hermitage in St. Petersburg.

PIONEER'S PELIK

The attic red-figured pellica is a clay artefact that dates back to the start of the fifth century BC. The artwork depicts a man, a boy, and another boy looking up towards a bird.

LEAGRA’S HYDRIA

The Attic Red Figure Hydria, or vase, is a famous artwork that depicts the funeral procession of the hero Hector. The vase was decorated by Greek artists during the Archaic Era and is made out of clay.
