WINTER PALACE
The Winter Palace in St. Petersburg is a magnificent architectural monument that has stood for centuries as the residence of the Russian rulers. It was built in the 18th century by the architect Bartolomeo Rastrelli and has since been used as a palace, museum, and venue for various events. During the 20th century, the palace was used as a prison and a military base, but in 1992 it was returned to its former glory as a cultural and tourist attraction. Today, it is one of the most visited tourist destinations in Russia, attracting millions of visitors from all over the world.
NEW HERMITAGE
The New Hermitage Museum was opened in Saint Petersburg, Russia, on December 17, 2019. The museum is a joint project of the Hermitage Foundation and the St. Petersburg State Museum Institution. It houses more than 300,000 items, including paintings, sculptures, furniture, and decorative arts. The New Hermitage is located in the historical building of the former Winter Palace, which was rebuilt and renovated to house the museum. The museum offers a variety of exhibitions, educational programs, and cultural events for visitors of all ages.
OLD HERMITAGE
The Old Hermitage Museum was founded in St. Petersburg in 1815 and has since become one of the oldest and most important museums in Russia. The collection of the museum includes paintings, sculptures, artifacts, and more from various cultures and eras. During World War II, the museum suffered significant damage from bombing and fire, but the collection was saved and preserved. Today, the museum continues to attract millions of visitors from around the world and offers a variety of educational programs and exhibitions.
